Why Regular Sleep Matters

Sleep isn’t just rest — it's essential to many bodily systems, mental health, and long-term well-being. Here’s what the research shows & how to improve your sleep.


Health Benefits of Good Sleep

  • Immune function: Proper sleep helps your immune system fight disease more effectively.
  • Memory & Learning: Sleep is vital for consolidating memories and improving learning.
  • Mood & Mental Health: Lack of sleep is linked to increased risk of anxiety, depression, mood swings.
  • Metabolic Health & Weight Management: Good sleep helps regulate appetite, hormones; reduces risk of obesity and diabetes.
  • Cardiovascular Health:

Sleep Recommendations

  • Aim for **7-9 hours** of quality sleep per night.
  • Keep a regular sleep schedule — go to bed and wake up at the same times daily.
  • Create a calming bedtime routine: limit screens 30 mins before bed, relax with reading or gentle stretching.
  • Optimize sleep environment: dark, cool, quiet. Use blackout curtains; ensure bed is comfortable.
  • Avoid heavy meals, caffeine, or stimulating activity close to bedtime.
  • Stay physically active during the day — exercise supports better sleep.

Sources: Harvard Medical School, SleepFoundation.org, UC Davis Health.
